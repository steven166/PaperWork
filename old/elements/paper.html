Paper Elements

Paper elements are like in the real world "3D". This means that everything is like paper: It is like a flat surface, but it can move in a 3D world and it's made of a material.

Flat surface

Flat surface means that a paper is always flat, it never bends or folds.

3D world

A paper element is always in a 3D world. It hovers "above" the screen. So if you hold a sheet of paper above a surface you see a shadow. If you move the paper up, the shadow grows bigger, and when you lower it the shadow shrinks.

Also if you move a sheet of paper in the real world, it does not jump from one point to anoter, it animate! You should also create a natural motion of you elements, so use animations! The best transition you can use is Swift Out instead of ease-in-out You should rather use Swift Out than ease-in-out

Swift Out (css: cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1))
Ease in out (css: ease-in-out)

It's made of a material

Google's Material Design specified that every sheet of paper has 1 color, so NO gradians. At the "Colors" tab you can see a full list of colors you can use for you elements

Usage

CSS class Description
paper Create transparent paper with shadow and padding
paper.round Create transparent paper with shadow, padding and round borders
paper.ani Add Swift Out transition to a sheet of paper
paper.ani Add Swift Out transition to a sheet of paper
paper-shadow-x Replace x with a number 0-5 for the size of the shadow
